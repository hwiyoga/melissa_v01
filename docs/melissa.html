Melissa

A semi-automated AI trading bot for Polymarket — scanning crypto up/down markets and short-window prediction markets, powered by Claude AI with real-time Binance price feeds.

Introduction

Melissa is a Python-based trading bot that monitors Polymarket prediction markets, identifies potential mispricing using AI analysis, and executes trades via the Polymarket CLOB API.

The bot combines three data sources to make decisions: Polymarket market prices (sentiment/positioning), Binance spot price data (real-time crypto direction), and Claude AI (reasoning, confidence scoring, and pattern recognition).

Architecture

Melissa is a single Python file (bot.py) with no external database. All state is stored in JSON files on disk. It runs as a background loop process on a local machine or VPS.

Step 1 — Fetch Markets

Melissa fetches active Polymarket markets in two categories:

  • Crypto up/down markets — 5-min and 15-min windows for BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP. Up to 5 upcoming windows per coin per timeframe.
  • Today's short-window markets — Any Polymarket event closing within 24 hours with volume > $200,000 (sports, news, weather, crypto price levels).

Step 2 — Detect Deviation

Claude is only called if there is a reason to. Markets are scanned for a price deviation threshold of >8¢ from 50/50 (e.g. one side at 58¢+). Markets near resolution (>90¢ or <10¢) are excluded. High-volume today's markets are always included regardless of deviation.

Step 3 — Fetch Binance + Polymarket Trends

Before sending data to Claude, Melissa enriches each coin section with:

  • Binance spot feed — Last 5 candles at 1-min intervals (5-min markets) or 3-min intervals (15-min markets). Shows real direction and velocity of the actual crypto price.
  • Polymarket price trend — Per-token price history within the current window from CLOB /prices-history. Shows how market sentiment has shifted.

Step 4 — Ask Claude

Claude receives a combined prompt containing:

  • All market data (prices, token IDs, Binance trends, Polymarket trends)
  • Recent trade performance history (last 20 resolved trades)
  • Learned lessons from past failures (lessons.md)

Claude returns a JSON array of trade recommendations, each with a confidence level (low, medium, high), a target price, limit price, and reasoning.

Step 5 — Approve & Execute

In auto mode, HIGH confidence trades execute immediately. Medium and low confidence trades are sent to Telegram for manual approval. In manual mode, all trades require approval via Telegram or terminal.

Market Scanning

Polymarket crypto up/down markets follow a predictable slug pattern: <coin>-updown-<duration>-<unix_timestamp>. Melissa generates these slugs from the current time, incrementing forward to find upcoming windows.

# Example slugs generated by Melissa
btc-updown-5m-1774800000
eth-updown-15m-1774800900
sol-updown-5m-1774800300

Markets that have already expired (end date in the past) are filtered out before being sent to Claude.

Signal Detection

Melissa uses a deviation gate to decide whether to invoke Claude. This prevents unnecessary API calls when markets are near 50/50.

ConditionAction
No crypto deviation >8¢ AND no high-volume today's marketSkip Claude, wait for next scan
Crypto deviation >8¢ detectedInclude that market in Claude's prompt
Today's market with volume >$200kAlways include in Claude's prompt
Market at >90¢ or <10¢Excluded (near resolution, no edge)
Note A deviation from 50/50 does NOT automatically mean a trade. It only triggers Claude to look. Claude then applies trend analysis and lessons to decide whether to act.

Binance Price Feed

Melissa fetches real-time spot price data from Binance's public klines API (no API key required). This is the primary signal for determining actual crypto price direction — independent of Polymarket's own lagged data.

What Claude Sees

BTC spot (Binance): FALLING ↓  $83,450 → $83,210 → $82,980  (-$470 in 4 min)
Polymarket sentiment:           Down 68¢ / Up 32¢
Polymarket trend (token):       FALLING ↓  47¢ → 51¢ → 68¢  (+21¢ in 8 min)

Decision Logic

BinancePolymarketInterpretationAction
FALLING ↓Down leadingMomentum confirmedDo NOT fade — skip or LOW only
RISING ↑Down leadingDivergence — Polymarket laggingPotential edge — investigate
FLAT →Any deviationStale signal, possible mean reversionMedium confidence acceptable
Key Insight The divergence case — where Binance is moving in the opposite direction to what Polymarket has priced — is where real alpha exists. Polymarket reacts slower than spot markets, creating brief windows of mispricing.

Claude AI Engine

Claude Opus 4.6 is Melissa's reasoning core. It receives all market data and returns structured trade recommendations as a JSON array.

System Prompt Policy

Claude is instructed to behave as a prediction market analyst with two market categories:

  • Crypto up/down markets — Be conservative. Check Binance direction before acting on any deviation. Never HIGH confidence on deviation alone.
  • Today's short-window markets — Sports, news, weather. Real edge exists from base rates and news context. More willing to go HIGH confidence.

Learning Loop

Claude's decision-making improves over time through two mechanisms:

  • Recent performance history — Last 20 resolved trades (wins/losses, P&L) are appended to every prompt so Claude calibrates its confidence based on what's actually been working.
  • lessons.md — A human-curated file of explicit rules derived from real trade failures. Injected into the system prompt every call.

Trade Execution

Live trades are placed via the Polymarket CLOB (Central Limit Order Book) API using py-clob-client. Orders are GTC (Good Till Cancelled) limit orders.

Execution Guards

  • Limit price must be between 0.01 and 0.99
  • Order size capped at $25 USDC hard limit
  • Failed orders (e.g. insufficient balance) are caught, logged, and reported to Telegram — bot continues without crashing

Trade Logging

Every executed trade (live or paper) is logged to a JSON file with full details including timestamp, token ID, price, shares, confidence, and reasoning.

  • live_trades.json — Live executed orders
  • paper_trades.json — Paper mode simulated orders

Safety Controls

PID Lock — No Duplicate Instances Melissa writes its process ID to /tmp/melissa_bot.pid on start. Any new start attempt checks this file — if the recorded PID is still alive, the new process exits immediately with an error. This prevents duplicate instances from multiplying API costs. Always use kill <PID> to stop Melissa cleanly.
Order Size Cap Even if Claude recommends a large position, the hard MAX_ORDER_SIZE = $25 cap applies at execution time. Orders above this are rejected before hitting the CLOB.
No VPN Required Polymarket's CLOB API returns 403 errors when accessed through a US-pointing VPN from certain regions. Run Melissa without VPN active from Jakarta / outside the US.
Crash Recovery Failed order placements (network errors, insufficient balance, API errors) are caught with try/except, reported to Telegram, and skipped — the loop continues without crashing.

Learned Lessons

These lessons are derived from real trade outcomes and injected into Claude's system prompt on every call.

Lesson 1
Crypto 5-min/15-min Up/Down Markets — Use the Price Trend
Deviation from 50/50 reflects real momentum, not mispricing. Active traders are already positioned based on live price movement. A market at 70¢ Down almost certainly means crypto IS falling.
RISING Binance + Down leading = momentum fade → SKIP
FLAT Binance + deviation = possible mean reversion → MEDIUM max
Binance contradicts Polymarket = divergence signal → investigate
Lesson 2
Sports Spread and News Markets — Real Edge Exists
Sports spread bets (Celtics -6.5) and news markets (DHS shutdown) have demonstrated profitability. Claude's knowledge of base rates, team form, and news context provides genuine information advantage over the market price.
Prioritize today's-market signals. Be more willing to flag HIGH confidence when mispricing is clear.
Lesson 3
Averaging Down on Losing Positions
The Indonesia trade deal was bought twice ($4 + $6 = $10 total), then sold for $3.52 — a $6.48 loss. The second buy averaged down on a position already moving against us.
Never buy more of a market where a position already exists at a worse price.
Lesson 4
Long-Tail Geopolitical Bets at Extreme Odds
France/UK/Germany strike Iran (Yes at 7¢) and Fed 50bps cut (Yes at 0.4¢) both expired worthless. Low-probability geopolitical bets are lottery tickets, not edges.
Avoid bets below 10¢ on geopolitical events unless there is a very specific, time-sensitive catalyst.
Lesson 5
Duplicate Instances — API Cost Explosion
On 2026-03-30, 7 Melissa instances ran simultaneously and consumed 70% of the Anthropic API budget in under a day (~$6.72/hour at peak). Each restart left orphaned background processes.
PID lock now enforced. Always run ps aux | grep bot.py before starting. Kill old instances explicitly.
